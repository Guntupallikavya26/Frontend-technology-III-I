Historical Monuments of India

Exploring the Rich Heritage

Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of Mumtaz Mahal. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of love.

Charminar, Hyderabad

The Charminar was built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah. It is famous for its four minarets and Indo-Islamic architecture.

Qutub Minar, Delhi

Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world's tallest brick minaret with a height of 72.5 meters.

Red Fort, Delhi

The Red Fort was built by Shah Jahan between 1638 and 1648. It represents Mughal architecture and India's history.

India Gate, Delhi

India Gate is a war memorial designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens to honor Indian soldiers.

Fatehpur Sikri, Agra

Fatehpur Sikri was the Mughal capital during Emperor Akbar's reign. It is known for its beautiful red sandstone architecture.

Meenakshi Amman Temple, Madurai

This temple is famous for its Dravidian architecture and beautifully carved gopurams.

Lepakshi, Andhra Pradesh

Lepakshi is famous for Veerabhadra Temple, hanging pillar, sculptures and Vijayanagara architecture.
